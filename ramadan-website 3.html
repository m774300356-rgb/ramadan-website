Foundation of Faith

The Five Pillars
of Islam

The five pillars are the core acts of worship that every Muslim is obligated to perform. They form the foundation upon which Islamic life is built — a framework of devotion, community, and submission to God.

☝️
01
الشَّهَادَة
Shahada

The declaration of faith: "There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is His messenger." The entry point to Islam.

🕌
02
الصَّلَاة
Salah

Five daily prayers performed at dawn, midday, afternoon, sunset, and night — keeping the believer connected to God.

💛
03
الزَّكَاة
Zakat

Obligatory almsgiving — 2.5% of accumulated wealth given annually to those in need, purifying one's wealth and soul.

🌙
04
الصَّوْم
Sawm

Fasting during Ramadan from dawn to sunset — abstaining from food, drink, and sin to achieve greater God-consciousness.

🕋
05
الحَجّ
Hajj

The pilgrimage to Mecca once in a lifetime for those who are able — the largest annual gathering of people on Earth.

Why Ramadan is the Greatest Month

Ramadan is the ninth month of the Islamic Hijri calendar — the month in which the Holy Quran was first revealed to Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. It is a month of intense worship, self-reflection, and community.

Muslims fast from Fajr (dawn) to Maghrib (sunset), abstaining from food, drink, and sinful behaviour. The fast is not merely physical — it is a purification of the soul, a training of willpower, and a reminder of the blessings God has given.

The last ten nights of Ramadan are especially sacred, containing Laylat al-Qadr — the Night of Power — which the Quran describes as better than a thousand months of worship.

🌅
Suhoor & IftarThe pre-dawn meal (Suhoor) and the breaking of fast at sunset (Iftar) are both highly recommended Sunnahs of the Prophet ﷺ.
📖
Tarawih PrayersSpecial nightly prayers in which the entire Quran is recited over the course of the month — a beloved tradition in mosques worldwide.
⭐
Laylat al-QadrThe Night of Power — sought in the last odd nights of Ramadan. Worship on this night equals over 83 years of continuous prayer.
Around the World

Beautiful Traditions
of Ramadan

🍽️
Daily Ritual
Iftar — Breaking the Fast
Families gather at sunset to break their fast, traditionally beginning with dates and water, followed by a full meal shared in gratitude and love.
📖
Spiritual Practice
Reciting the Holy Quran
Many Muslims aim to complete the entire Quran during Ramadan, dedicating extra hours each day to its recitation and reflection.
🤲
Community
Sadaqah & Giving
Charity multiplies in reward during Ramadan. Mosques and families organize community iftars to feed those in need.
🕌
Night Prayer
Tarawih at the Mosque
After Isha, mosques fill with worshippers for Tarawih — a beautiful collective prayer that continues late into the night.
🎉
Celebration
Eid al-Fitr
The joyous feast that follows Ramadan — new clothes, gifts, sweet dishes, and prayers of gratitude for completing the blessed month.
📿
Sunnah
Dates & Dhikr
Breaking fast with dates follows the Sunnah of the Prophet ﷺ. Prayer beads (Misbaha) are used to glorify God through the day.

Essential Duas
of Ramadan

اللَّهُمَّ إِنَّكَ عَفُوٌّ تُحِبُّ الْعَفْوَ فَاعْفُ عَنِّي
"O Allah, You are Pardoning, You love to pardon, so pardon me."

The most recommended dua for Laylat al-Qadr, taught by the Prophet ﷺ to Aisha (RA). Simple, powerful, and profound.

Laylat al-Qadr
اللَّهُمَّ لَكَ صُمْتُ وَبِكَ آمَنْتُ وَعَلَيْكَ تَوَكَّلْتُ وَعَلَى رِزْقِكَ أَفْطَرْتُ
"O Allah, for You I fasted, in You I believe, upon You I rely, and with Your provision I break my fast."

The dua recited at the time of breaking the fast each evening — a moment of deep gratitude and surrender.

Iftar Time
نَوَيْتُ صَوْمَ غَدٍ عَنْ أَدَاءِ فَرْضِ شَهْرِ رَمَضَانَ هٰذِهِ السَّنَةِ لِلّٰهِ تَعَالٰى
"I intend to fast tomorrow to fulfil the obligation of the month of Ramadan this year for the sake of Allah."

The intention (niyyah) for the Ramadan fast, renewed each night before Suhoor — a conscious act of devotion.

Niyyah — Before Suhoor
رَبِّ اغْفِرْ لِي وَتُبْ عَلَيَّ إِنَّكَ أَنْتَ التَّوَّابُ الرَّحِيمُ
"My Lord, forgive me and accept my repentance. Truly, You are the Ever-Accepting of Repentance, the Most Merciful."

A short but powerful dua of repentance recommended to be recited abundantly throughout the month of Ramadan.
